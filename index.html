FAQ

What types of milk you can add to a coffee?
You can add to coffee dairy and non-dairy milks.
What is an origin of a dairy milk?
Dairy milks are derived from the mammary glands of mammals, typically cows, goats, and sheep. They are also commonly referred to as "animal milks" because they come from animals.
What is an origin of a non-dairy milk?
Non-dairy milks, also known as plant-based milks, are milk alternatives made from plant sources rather than animal milk. They are suitable for people who are lactose intolerant, have dairy allergies, follow a vegan diet, or simply prefer plant-based options.
Examples of dairy milks
- Cow's Milk: This is the most widely consumed dairy milk. It comes in various forms, including whole milk, 2% milk, 1% milk, and skim milk, each with different levels of fat content.
- Goat's Milk: Goat's milk is similar to cow's milk but has a distinctive flavor and is easier for some people to digest.
- Sheep's Milk: Sheep's milk is less common but is used to make specialty cheeses and yogurt.
Examples of non-dairy milks
- Almond Milk: Made from almonds, almond milk has a nutty flavor and is available in sweetened and unsweetened varieties.
- Soy Milk: Made from soybeans, soy milk is a popular choice due to its neutral flavor and nutritional content. It's often fortified with vitamins and minerals.
- Oat Milk: Made from oats, oat milk is known for its creamy texture and natural sweetness.
- Coconut Milk: Made from coconuts, coconut milk has a tropical flavor and is available in both canned and carton forms.
- Rice Milk: Made from milled rice, rice milk is mildly sweet and has a thin consistency.
- Hemp Milk: Made from hemp seeds, hemp milk has a slightly nutty flavor and is a good source of omega-3 fatty acids.
- Cashew Milk: Made from cashews, cashew milk is creamy and has a mild, nutty taste.
- Macadamia Milk: Made from macadamia nuts, this milk has a rich, buttery flavor.
